# UnclaimedProperty AI — Pain Points

## Primary Pain Point
> **"I might have money sitting in a state treasury from an old bank account or utility deposit, but I don't know how to find it or how to file the claim forms."**

### 1. Fragmentation
- **Problem:** Every US state has its own separate treasury database.
- **Scale:** $70 Billion is currently held in state unclaimed property programs.
- **Average Claim:** $2,080.
- **Friction:** Users have to search 50+ websites manually if they've moved across states.

### 2. Complex Verification
- **Problem:** Proving ownership for a 10-year-old account at an address you no longer live at is hard.
- **Scale:** Most claim forms are 5-10 page PDFs requiring notarization, proof of address, and specific ID formats.

### 3. Escheatment Ignorance
- **Problem:** Most people don't even know what "escheatment" is or that the state can take their money if an account is inactive.

### 4. Scammer Fear
- **Problem:** Unclaimed property is a huge target for scammers. Users are wary of "finder services" that take 50%.
- **Our Solution:** A trusted, transparent AI service with a flat $29 fee vs. the predatory 50% contingency fees.

## User Personas
- **"The Cross-Country Mover"**: Lived in 4 states in 10 years; almost certainly has a utility deposit or old check somewhere.
- **"The Heir"**: Managing a parent's estate; needs to find all forgotten assets across the country.
- **"The Skeptical Frugalist"**: Wants every penny they're owed but hates paperwork.

## Current Alternatives
1. **MissingMoney.com**: The official multi-state search (incomplete data, still requires manual filing per state).
2. **State Treasury Websites**: Free but requires 50 manual searches and 50 different form types.
3. **Contingency "Finders"**: Charge 30-50% of the recovered amount.
4. **Nothing**: The money stays with the state forever (escheats).
